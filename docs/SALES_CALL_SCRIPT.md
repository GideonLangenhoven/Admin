# BookingTours — 20-Minute Sales Call Script

> For the person running a discovery/demo call with a prospective operator.
> Goal: understand their pain, show them the platform solves it, and close with a clear next step.

---

## Before the Call (prep)

- Open the **live demo dashboard** (your own account or a demo tenant)
- Have the **booking site** open in a second tab
- Know their business name and what activity they run (check their website/Instagram before the call)
- Have pricing ready: R1,500/month starter, pause anytime, no lock-in

---

## Minute 0–3 — Build Rapport & Qualify (3 min)

**Don't pitch yet.** Ask questions and listen.

> "Thanks for jumping on. Before I show you anything — I want to understand how you're running things today so I only show you what's actually relevant."

### Questions to ask:

| # | Question | What you're listening for |
|---|----------|--------------------------|
| 1 | "Talk me through what happens when a customer wants to book with you right now." | Manual process? WhatsApp back-and-forth? Phone calls? Google Forms? |
| 2 | "How do you track your bookings and availability?" | Spreadsheets? Paper? Another system? Double-bookings? |
| 3 | "How do customers pay?" | Cash on the day? EFT? Payment links? No-shows? |
| 4 | "What happens when someone needs to cancel or reschedule?" | Manual admin? Refund headaches? Lost revenue? |
| 5 | "Do you run multiple tours or time slots per day?" | Capacity management pain? Shared equipment/guides? |
| 6 | "How do you communicate with customers after they book?" | Manual WhatsApps? Forgetting to send reminders? |
| 7 | "What's your biggest headache right now?" | Let them tell you — this is the pain you'll solve in the demo |

### Qualify while you listen:

- **Good fit**: They run scheduled activities, take bookings, struggle with admin
- **Not a fit**: They're a restaurant, retail shop, or don't take advance bookings (be honest, save everyone's time)

**Transition:**

> "Got it. So the biggest pain is [repeat their #7 back to them]. Let me show you exactly how we handle that — I'll keep it quick, only the stuff that matters to you."

---

## Minute 3–15 — The Demo (12 min)

**Rule: Only show features that solve the pain they just told you about.** Don't demo everything. Pick 5–6 features max from the menu below based on their answers.

### Demo Menu — Pick What's Relevant

---

#### If they said: "Bookings are chaos / I use spreadsheets"
**Show: Dashboard + New Booking (2 min)**

> "This is your dashboard. Every morning you open this and see today's pax count, pending refunds, unread messages — everything in one place."

- Show the stats cards (Today's Pax, Pending Refunds, Inbox Action)
- Click **+ New Booking**
- Walk through: pick tour, pick date, select slot from the availability matrix
- Show the colour-coded capacity grid

> "No more spreadsheets. No more double-bookings. Every slot knows exactly how many seats are left."

---

#### If they said: "Customers WhatsApp me and I have to reply manually"
**Show: WhatsApp Chatbot + Inbox (2 min)**

> "This is the one that changes everything for most operators."

- Open **Inbox** — show the unified WhatsApp + web chat view
- Explain the chatbot:

> "When a customer sends you a WhatsApp, our chatbot handles the entire booking conversation — tour selection, date picking, payment link — automatically. You only step in for edge cases. Most operators save 2–3 hours a day."

- Show a sample conversation if one exists
- Mention: *"It also handles rescheduling and cancellations. Customer types 'reschedule' and the bot walks them through it."*

---

#### If they said: "No-shows and payment chasing are killing me"
**Show: Payment Flow + Hold System (2 min)**

> "When you create a booking as PENDING, the system automatically sends the customer a secure Yoco payment link via email AND WhatsApp. If they don't pay within your hold window — 24 hours by default — the slot releases automatically."

- Create a PENDING booking and show the payment link email
- Show the hold duration selector (2h, 6h, 12h, 24h, 48h)

> "No more chasing. No more holding seats for people who never pay."

---

#### If they said: "I have multiple tours / shared equipment"
**Show: Tours + Shared Resources (2 min)**

- Open **Settings > Tours & Activities** — show multiple tours
- Open **Settings > Shared Resources**

> "Say you have 10 kayaks shared between your morning and sunset tours. When 6 get booked on the morning slot, the sunset automatically shows only 4 available. No manual tracking."

- Show a resource linked to two tours

> "This is something most booking systems can't do. They treat each tour as isolated."

---

#### If they said: "Bad weather days are a nightmare"
**Show: Weather Monitor (1 min)**

- Open **Weather** page

> "You set your wind speed and rain thresholds. When weather crosses those limits, the system can auto-cancel affected bookings, notify every customer via email and WhatsApp, and offer them a reschedule link — all without you touching anything."

> "Most operators tell me weather days used to take 2 hours of phone calls. Now it's zero."

---

#### If they said: "I want to grow but can't handle more admin"
**Show: Automated Communications (2 min)**

> "Once a booking is confirmed, the system handles everything automatically."

Walk through the sequence:
1. **Booking confirmation** — email + WhatsApp with all details, waiver link, Google Maps
2. **Day-before reminder** — WhatsApp reminder with what to bring
3. **Post-trip review request** — asks for a Google/TripAdvisor review
4. **Re-engagement** — if they haven't booked in X days, sends a "we miss you" message

> "You set it up once. It runs forever. Your repeat booking rate goes up without you doing anything."

---

#### If they said: "I want to sell vouchers / do promotions"
**Show: Vouchers + Promo Codes (2 min)**

- Open **Vouchers** — show the voucher list
- Explain: *"Customers buy gift vouchers on your booking page. They get a beautiful email with a code. When someone redeems it, the balance drains automatically — even partial redemption works."*
- Open **Marketing > Promotions** — show promo code creation
- Explain: *"Create codes like SUMMER20 for 20% off. Set usage limits, expiry dates, minimum order amounts. Customers apply them at checkout."*

> "This is built in. No Mailchimp. No third-party voucher service. No extra cost."

---

#### If they said: "I need to look professional"
**Show: Customer Booking Experience (2 min)**

- Switch to the **booking site** tab
- Walk through the customer flow: pick tour → pick date → pick slot → enter details → add-ons → promo code → voucher → pay

> "This is what your customers see. Your branding, your colours, your logo. Works perfectly on mobile. Takes under 60 seconds to book."

- Show the **self-service portal**: customer enters email + phone, gets OTP, can view/reschedule/cancel their own bookings

> "Customers manage themselves. No more 'can you check my booking?' phone calls."

---

#### Always show (30 seconds each):

**Digital Waivers**
> "Waivers are sent automatically after booking. Customers sign on their phone. You see the status on each booking — signed or pending. No paper. No clipboard."

**Invoices**
> "Every booking gets a tax invoice PDF generated and emailed automatically. Your accountant will love you."

**Marketing Engine**
> "Built-in email campaigns. Import your contacts, build templates with our drag-and-drop editor, track opens and clicks. No Mailchimp subscription needed."

## Minute 15–17 — Pricing & Objection Handling (2 min)

Transition naturally:

> "So that's the platform. Any questions about what you've seen?"

Answer their questions, then:

> "Pricing is simple. R1,500 a month. That includes everything you've just seen — unlimited bookings, unlimited tours, WhatsApp chatbot, marketing, the lot. If you need extra admin logins, it's R750 each. No setup fee. No contracts."

### Common Objections

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "That's expensive" | "What are you spending on Mailchimp + a booking plugin + a voucher service + WhatsApp Business API separately? Most operators are paying more for less and stitching it together with spreadsheets." |
| "I need to think about it" | "Totally fair. What specifically do you want to think through? I might be able to answer it now." |
| "Does it integrate with X?" | "What does X do for you today? Let me check if we handle that natively — most of the time we do." |
| "What about off-season?" | "You can pause your subscription anytime. No charge while you're paused. Unpause when bookings start again." |
| "I'm locked into another system" | "When does that end? I can set everything up in advance so you're ready to switch on day one." |
| "Can I try it first?" | "Absolutely. I can set up your account right now and you can play with it for a week. No charge until you're ready to go live." |
| "I'm not tech-savvy" | "The onboarding call takes 5 minutes. I set up your tours and first booking with you. After that, if you can use WhatsApp, you can use this." |

---

## Minute 17–19 — Close (2 min)

**Go for the next step. Don't leave it open-ended.**

### If they're ready:

> "Want me to set up your account right now? Takes 5 minutes. I'll create your business, add your first tour, and you'll have a working booking page before we hang up."

→ If yes: transition straight to the [5-minute onboarding call script](./ONBOARDING_CALL_SCRIPT.md)

### If they need time:

> "No problem. Here's what I suggest — I'll send you a link to our booking page so you can see the customer experience yourself. Try making a test booking. Then let's jump on a 5-minute call this week to get you set up. What day works?"

→ Book the follow-up before hanging up. Don't say "I'll send you info and you can get back to me."

### If they're not a fit:

> "Honestly, it sounds like [reason] might mean this isn't the right tool for you right now. If that changes, you know where to find me."

→ Be direct. Don't waste their time or yours.

## Minute 19–20 — Wrap Up (1 min)

> "Thanks for your time, [name]. To recap:
> - [Repeat the main pain they mentioned]
> - [How the platform solves it]
> - [The next step you agreed on]
>
> I'll send you a WhatsApp with the booking page link and my direct number. Looking forward to getting you set up."

---

## Key Principles

1. **Listen more than you talk.** The first 3 minutes are theirs. The demo is just answering their problems with a screen.
2. **Only demo what they care about.** Showing 12 features to someone who just wants online payments is a waste of their attention.
3. **Show, don't tell.** Click buttons. Create a booking. Show the email that arrives. Real actions beat slides.
4. **Close with a specific next step.** "I'll send you info" is not a next step. "Tuesday at 10am for setup" is.
5. **Be honest about fit.** Turning away a bad-fit prospect builds more trust than forcing a sale.
