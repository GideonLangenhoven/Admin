# CapeKayak Ads Playbook v1

## Objective
Generate qualified operator demos and paid onboarding starts while maintaining predictable customer acquisition cost.

## Offer to Advertise
- Core hook: all features included from day one.
- Price clarity:
  - Starter: R1,500/month + R3,500 once-off setup
  - Growth: R3,000/month + R3,500 once-off setup
  - Pro: R6,500/month + R3,500 once-off setup
- Add-on clarity:
  - Landing page build: R3,500 first page
  - Additional pages: R1,500 per page
  - Hosting: R500/month when landing page add-on is active

## Budget Tiers (Monthly)
- Lean test (`R15,000`): validate creative and audience fit before scaling.
- Core launch (`R30,000`): stable learning with enough data for weekly optimization.
- Growth push (`R60,000+`): scale only after conversion path is proven.

## Recommended Channel Split
- `40%` Meta (Facebook/Instagram): demand capture + retargeting.
- `40%` Google Search: high-intent queries around booking software.
- `20%` LinkedIn: operator-owner and manager persona testing.

## Campaign Structure

### Meta
- Campaign 1: Prospecting (broad + interest clusters by tourism/activity operators).
- Campaign 2: Retargeting (site visitors, video viewers, pricing-page visitors).
- Optimization event: qualified lead or booked demo.

### Google Search
- Campaign 1: high-intent non-brand terms (`booking software`, `tour operator booking system`, `activity booking management`).
- Campaign 2: competitor and alternative framing (with compliant ad copy).
- Campaign 3: brand defense terms (CapeKayak and brand variants).
- Match types: exact + phrase first, broad only after search term quality is stable.

### LinkedIn
- Single campaign with tight geography + role filters.
- Objective: lead generation form or website demo booking.
- Use only if ICP fit and lead quality justify higher CPC.

## Creative and Messaging Matrix
- Angle 1: "All features from day one"
- Angle 2: "Scale seats and paid bookings only when you grow"
- Angle 3: "From inquiry to paid booking to operations in one system"
- Angle 4: "Predictable add-ons: landing page build and hosting clearly priced"

## Landing Flow
- Cold traffic destination: `/operators`
- Objection handling path:
  - `/case-study/cape-kayak`
  - `/compare/manual-vs-disconnected-tools`
- CTA hierarchy:
  - Primary: book demo
  - Secondary: start onboarding
  - Tertiary: request landing page add-on quote

## Tracking and Attribution (Required)
- Google Ads conversion tracking with enhanced conversions enabled.
- Meta Pixel with Conversions API integration.
- LinkedIn Insight Tag with lead-event tracking.
- UTM standards on every ad:
  - `utm_source`, `utm_medium`, `utm_campaign`, `utm_content`, `utm_term`
- Weekly join-up:
  - ad platform conversions
  - CRM demo outcomes
  - paid booking conversion from product data

## Optimization Cadence
- Days 1-3: creative validation (keep spend broad, watch CTR and CPL trends).
- Days 4-7: cut lowest-performing creatives and audience segments.
- Days 8-14: shift budget to best performing campaign/ad set pairs.
- Week 3+: scale budget by 20-30% only when lead quality is stable.

## Guardrails
- Do not scale spend if demos are rising but paid onboarding is flat.
- Pause any campaign that misses qualified lead threshold for 5 consecutive days.
- Enforce 24-hour sales response SLA for all paid leads.

## Initial Benchmarks to Track
- CTR by channel and creative.
- Cost per qualified demo.
- Demo attendance rate.
- Demo-to-paid onboarding rate.
- Paid booking conversion rate (north-star).

## What Makes Ads Work for This Launch
- Offer clarity is non-negotiable: include setup fee and recurring pricing in ad-to-page flow.
- Fast follow-up is a multiplier: ad spend without sales speed wastes budget.
- Retargeting should start on day one, not after launch week.
